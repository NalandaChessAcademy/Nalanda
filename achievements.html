Achievements

Our students participated in various district, state, National and International Level Tournaments every year and we are proud to say that many of them have emerged as Champions. Hundred + counting students are getting international FIDE rating every month from our academy.

our students

Three of our students have been conferred the prestigious Ekalavya Award. They are:

Sriram Sarja (2007), Stany G A (2010) and Yashas.D(2014)

1.Sriram Sarja (2007)

He was awarded with ‘Ekalavya award’ by Government of Karnataka. He won so many prizes in State level, National level and international level.

He represented India for international chess festival in under-15 age category and also represented Bangalore city in Asianchampionship in Lebenon and Philiphines in 2011.

2.Stany G A (2010)

Currently he is one of the International Master of chess. Government of Karnataka recognized him for his achievements and awarded with ‘Ekalavya Award’ He was the national champion in the year 2010 (Under-17 age category).

He was Asian silver Medalist in Under-18 age category in Indo- nesia. He won gold medal in AsianYouth champi- onship in China. He won 4th place in Under-18 international Championship held in Brazil (2011).

3.Yashas.D(2014)

He won so many state and national chess champi- onships, He won Gold medal in commonwealth championship (under-16 age category) in 2010 at Singapore, In sub-junior group he represented India at Turky and he won silver medal, He won gold medal in Asian chess championship at China in 2010.

4.Shristi J. Shetty

She won silver medal in Commonwealth champi- onship in 2007 (under-14 age group). She repre- sented India in different International tournaments at Spain in 2009 [Expossure trip]. She won several Gold medals in SGFI [School games federation of India].

5. Kishan Gangolli

He represented India for *chess olampaid for visually impared and blinds* at Chennai in 2012 and won Gold medal in the event. He won so many prizes in district, state, national and international level tournaments. He selected in the Indian team for the World cup in 2013 in Spain. Kishan Gangolli (India) has won the Gold Medal at Asian Chess Championship 2017 for Visually Challenged.
