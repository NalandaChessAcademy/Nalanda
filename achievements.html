ACHIEVEMENTS

Path towards Grandmastery

We aim to create a global Chess Education system to support the growth of Chess, create an awareness about the game through different modes of outreach programs and increase the value of educational chess amongst localities. Our students have participated and won many prestigious tournaments in the District, State and National Level Chess Championships.

Every month we have students emerging as Chess Champions with FIDE ratings. Highly acclaimed Chess coaches from Nalanda Academy are seasoned arbiters and invited to evaluate Chess Tournaments conducted across our nation. Founders of Nalanda Academy are revolutionising Chess Coaching by introducing technology that will accelerate player’s development.

1.STANY G A (2010)

Grandmaster Stany hails from Shivamogga started playing chess at the age of 8, joined Nalanda Chess Academy in the city where he was trained under Srikrishna Udupa. He made his 3rd and final IM norm in 4th Rethymno International Chess Tournament in Rethymno, Greece from July 7 to 14 2012. Earlier he had won silver medal in under-18 Asian Chess Championship of 2011 held in Philippines and had secured fourth position in Under 18 World Chess Championship held in the same year in Brazil.

2.SRIRAM SARJA (2007)

Ekalavya awardee recipient Sriram Sarja has won many prizes in State level, National level and International levels. He has represented India in the international Chess festival in under-15 age category and also represented Bangalore city in Asian Championship in Lebanon and the Philippines in 2011.

3.YASHAS.D(2014)

In the under 16 age category, Yashan has won Gold medal in Commonwealth Championship held in Singapore, 2010. In the sub-junior group he represented India at Turkey and he won silver medal, He won the gold medal in Asian Chess Championship at China in 2010.

4.SHRISTI J. SHETTY

In the under 14 age category, Shristi has bagged the silver medal in the Commonwealth Championship in 2017. She also represented India in different International tournaments at Spain in 2009 [Exposure trip]. She won several Gold medals in SGFI [School games federation of India].

5. KISHAN GANGOLLI

The five-time National blind champion, current Asian champion and two-time Olympiad medal winner, Kishan Gangolli is an esteemed student of Nalanda Academy.Credited with a FIDE rating of 1996 (standard), 2042 (rapid) and 2006 (blitz), the Shimoga scholar has represented Karnataka at the National School Games. He is selected in the Indian team for the World Cup in 2013 in Spain. He has won the Gold Medal at Asian Chess Championship 2017 for Visually Challenged.
